A Little History to This Much-celebrated Indian Attire

The saree is one of the most elegant forms of Indian attire. Unlike any other garment, this nine-yard wonder brings out the beauty of a woman and is a must-have attire in your wardrobe. This is one of the oldest forms of unstitched drape clothing to exist on this planet. The history of drapery clothing similar to the saree (साड़ी) dates back to the Indus Valley Civilization during 2800- 1800 B.C. The word 'saree' evolved from the Sanskrit word 'Saatikaa' which literally means women's attire. The saree is an elegant, sensuous and glamorous piece of clothing for women. They are also a canvas for printers and weavers to create artistic weaves, embroideries and jewelled embellishments. The saree is an incredibly versatile attire that hugs the contours of a woman’s body in such an aesthetic manner. Whether young or old, any woman can don a saree and look astounding. Unlike any other attire, a saree is the only traditional outfit that allows you to style it in various ways.

A Perfect Balance of Style and Culture

Indians were always good with their hands. When it came to hand-printed or handmade embroidered detailing, Indians were always the winner. In the olden days, women who wore fabrics made out of silk, muslin and cotton, were considered extraordinarily beautiful. Fabrics were specially woven for festive occasions and religious ceremonies that acquired specific names. Back then, the Indian literature prose and poems were also inspired by how ravishing and elegant the attires worn by the upper classes were. Some historical records say the silks and muslins back then were supremely expensive and they were sold to the ends of the world that included the people who lived around the Mediterranean Sea. Depending upon the texture and origin of the fabrics, many names were given to these fabrics. For instance, Chinnavastra was a fabric similar to Chinese silk. Kausheya was silk made from the most exquisite cocoons. Tasara silk, now known as Tassar or Tussar silk, was made using a special kind of shuttle. Moreover, the Moghuls wore exquisite quality brocades that were described as ‘Golden Dream’ or Kinkhwab. The Europeans, back then, were known for importing this fabric and calling it Kinkob. To this day, the brocade is known by this name in most European countries.

Tips on How to Look Slim in Sarees

How to look slim in sarees you ask? There are quite a few ways to solve this problem. As we know that sarees have been one of the most versatile pieces of clothing in the world, they are also not that hard to style. With the new trends hitting the market off late, wearing your sarees and looking slim is child’s play.
Firstly, make sure your saree is made from a light fabric. Something with intricate embroidery will not be as lightweight as a georgette or a chiffon printed saree. The thinner and lighter the fabric, the better it is to drape it around your body. You will find brands like Yashika, Saara, Pisara, VJ Fashions, Crally, etc., that offer you a vast collection of crepe, chiffon and georgette sarees.
The second tip is to choose dark colours. Dark colours make a big difference when you want to look slim. You can either choose a solid coloured saree or a printed one. If you go for the printed option, make sure the prints are light and elongated. Vertical prints do a better job of making you look slim rather than horizontal prints.
The third tip is to choose sarees that have thin borders. Thick borders with embroidery and embellishments may look glamorous and eye-catching, but when it comes to looking thin, then you must know that thin borders are the real deal. The fourth and the last point is long-sleeved blouses make you look thinner as compared to sleeveless blouses.
